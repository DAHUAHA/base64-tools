What is Unix Timestamp?

Unix timestamp (also called Epoch time) is the number of seconds that have elapsed since January 1, 1970 00:00:00 UTC. It's widely used in programming, databases, and APIs because it's easy to store and compare. Many languages and systems use milliseconds (10-digit) instead of seconds (10-digit vs 13-digit).
